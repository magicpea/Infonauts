Women earn only 18% of computer
science bachelors in the US.*

Let's unpack that.

Test

The study, led by Accenture and Girls who
Code, showed that 50% of women abandon
technology careers by the age of 35 and
that women are leaving tech roles at a 45%
higher rate than men. Only 21% of women
in the study said they believed the
technology industry was a place they could
thrive; sadly, that number falls
precipitously to 8% for women of color.

Let's take a look at the data.

As of 2020, 12% of the tech positions in the U.S.
are held by women of color. To break it down further,
women who identified as Asian or Pacific Islander
hold 7%, those who identified as Black or African
American hold 3%, and women who identified as Latina
or Hispanic hold 2% of all tech positions in the U.S.
Women who identified as white hold 13% of all tech
jobs in the U.S.
